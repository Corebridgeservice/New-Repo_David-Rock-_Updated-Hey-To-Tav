About

The story behind the work

Let me share a little with you about who is actually behind all of this.

I am a husband, a father, a community builder, and a lifelong learner. For as long as I can remember, I have been passionate about studying people. Not to sort them into types. To truly see them. What wakes someone up in the morning. What quietly holds them back. What happens in a person’s face the moment they finally recognize something true about themselves that no one had ever put into words. I have spent most of my life chasing that moment: in long conversations, in books by the hundreds, in the slow work of paying close attention to whoever is in front of me.

Underneath all of it is a single conviction: that you were made on purpose, with a beautiful design meant as a blessing to the world, by Someone who does not waste a detail. I believe God spoke the world into being, and that He knit each of us together with incredible care, down to every aspect of how we are wired. You do not have to share that belief to receive something of value here. But it is where the warmth, the passion, the creativity, and the best of who I am and all I co-create in partnership with our Creator come from, and I would rather tell you that plainly, right at the start, than leave it unsaid.

I should also tell you what I am not. I am not writing to you from the far side of a finished life. I carry a great deal of intensity, and I have spent years learning to point it toward growth, contribution, and relationship, rather than letting it spill onto the people I love. I know the distance between the person I want to be and the person I actually was this morning, and I work at closing it, slowly, the way everyone has to. If anything I make is any good, it is because I am still walking the same road I am inviting you onto, not because I reached the end of it and have “arrived.”

That is why this place exists. Everything here, the writing, the teachings, the Guide to Self, comes from one desire: to help you see how you were made, and what you were made for, clearly enough to build an even more beautiful life on what you see. I am not trying to be your authority in any way. I am trying to be a steady, warm presence who sees you clearly and hands you a map of yourself.

How I was shaped

It may help to know how I was shaped for this, because most of it was not my own doing.

I was homeschooled by my mother from first grade until college, and I credit her, more than any school I attended afterward, with what I am most grateful for. She did not teach me to find an answer and stop. She nurtured the part of me that asks, and seeks, and knocks, and keeps knocking until the door actually opens. That refusal to quit before I have found has run underneath everything since.

There was another root that runs deep. As a boy I loved the Messianic Jewish synagogue we attended, and the Hebrew way of reading Scripture, and of seeing God, became home soil long before I was old enough to choose it. I have been digging in that ground my whole life. Words spoken aloud carry a power of their own, and one line of liturgy, recited every Shabbat, slowly wove itself into the fabric of what I believe: let us learn in order to do; let us learn in order to teach. I have come to believe that is the right order, and that the order matters. You learn, and then you do; you walk the truth into your own life before you try to hand it to anyone else. What you have lived is often the most powerful gift you can give. And what you give that way does not shrink. It multiplies as it moves.

I grew up with very little technology, and then walked into rigorous training and a world that began changing faster than anyone expected. Looking back, the odd pairing feels less like accident and more like preparation: enough quiet, early on, to learn how to think, then enough rigor to learn how to think well, and now a place to stand in a moment when a great many people are going to need help remembering who they are. I do not say that with any grandiosity. I say it the way the servant counts what he was handed, as talents I did not earn and am not willing to bury.

I believe the curiosity and the training and the years were given on purpose, the way good seed is given to good ground, and that the reason you are handed anything is so you can give it away. What I most want to do with the rest of my life is invest what I was given in helping people see how they were made, so that more of them can do the same for the people they love. That is the work. That is the small corner of the kingdom I am trying to help build in this season of my life.

The practical background, for those who want it

For anyone who likes to know the ground a person stands on, the curiosity left a paper trail. I hold a bachelor’s degree from Western Washington University, and a law degree from Seattle University School of Law, where I sharpened my God-given talents to think rigorously and argue carefully. The formal list of degrees matters far less than the appetite behind it. I have pursued what I wanted to understand into classrooms and seminars across the country and around the world, gathering more certifications and trainings than are worth listing here, not for the letters and credentials they add to a name, but because I have never been able to leave a question alone once it has hold of me. All of it points the same direction. I learn as part of becoming the man He has called me to be, and so that I have more to give.

So that is me. A man who is still becoming, who believes you were made with intention, and who would count it an honor to help you see it. Whenever you are ready, the door is open.

If you want what I write to find you when it ships, leave your email here. Teachings, tools, and letters, sent as they are ready. Nothing else comes with it.
