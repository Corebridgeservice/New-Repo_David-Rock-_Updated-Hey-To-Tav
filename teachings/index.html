Teachings

Written to be read slowly

I write the way you walk a trail rather than drive past it. Some walks are long and some are short. The distance is set by what there is to see, not by a rule I keep. What does not change is the pace, unhurried, paying attention, in no hurry to reach the point before you have seen the thing for yourself. Many of these begin in the Hebrew and end in your kitchen. Some begin somewhere else and wander home by a different road.

Take what lands. Leave what does not. And if something perplexes you along the way, pay attention to that. In my experience, perplexed marks the spot.
