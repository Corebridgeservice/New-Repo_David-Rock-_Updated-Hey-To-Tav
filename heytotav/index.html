From Hey to Tav

The Reveal, the Covenant, and the Walk Between Them

Before the two letters, a word about why letters at all

I come at this as a person of faith, so let me tell you where I am standing before I ask you to look at anything. I believe God spoke the world into being, that reality itself began as something said out loud. And I believe the book that grew from that, the Hebrew Scriptures, was given with that same care, down to the letter, every word chosen on purpose by a God who does not waste a syllable. If that is true, then the shapes of the oldest letters are not decoration. They are design. They carry meaning the way everything He makes carries meaning.

You do not have to believe any of that to keep reading. I am only telling you where the richness comes from, so that when I show you two ancient letters and say they hold a whole way of living, you can see why I would look there. Watch the pattern first. You can decide for yourself what to make of the One who set it there.

There is a moment, when you finally see something true about yourself, that feels like arriving. The fog lifts, the pattern comes clear, and you think, there it is, that is who I am. Hold on to that feeling, because I want to show you something. That moment of seeing is not the end of the road. In the language God chose for His own Word, it is the first of two letters.

The first letter: hey

The Hebrew letter hey is the letter of revelation. In the oldest form of the writing, before the letters became the square shapes a Hebrew Bible uses today, hey was a picture. As Dr. Frank Seekins reads these ancient letter-pictures, it was drawn as a person standing with both arms lifted high, the figure of someone who has just seen something and cannot hold it in. Behold. Look. See what is being shown.

That is what revelation feels like, because that is what revelation is. Something that was always true gets lifted up where you can finally see it. You did not invent it. You did not earn it by being clever enough to notice it. It was there the whole time, and grace pulled back the curtain. The arms go up on their own.

The second letter: tav

But Hebrew does not leave you at the seeing. There is a second letter, and it changes what the first one is for.

The letter tav is the letter of covenant. In the oldest writing it was drawn as two sticks crossed over each other, a simple mark, the kind a person makes to seal an agreement. This is signed. This is settled. This is mine. Tav is the sign that seals.

And here is the quiet, staggering thing the language does with these two letters. A word that stands on its own often ends in hey, the reveal. The moment that word binds itself to something, it can change its ending to tav, the covenant. The freestanding word for fear, yirah, ends in hey. The moment it binds to God and becomes the fear of the LORD, yirat YHWH, it ends in tav. The word for the walking, halachah, ends in hey. The word for the daily steps you actually take, halakhot, ends in tav. The reveal becomes the covenant. The thing shown becomes the thing sealed. The road taught becomes the road walked, one step at a time.

God did not have to build His own grammar this way. He did it on purpose, because He is the kind of Father who tells you the same true thing in a hundred quiet ways until you cannot miss it.

The covenant is in the steps

Now hold the two letters next to a sentence from the book of James. Faith without works is dead (James 2:17). For a long time people have read that as a hard saying, a bar to measure up to. It is not. It is the same thing the Hebrew letters have been saying all along.

A truth that is revealed and never walked is a hey with no tav. A road shown to you that you never set foot on. Faith that has seen and will not move is the reveal with the seal left blank. But faith that takes a step, that lets what it has seen become how it lives, is the hey becoming the tav. The covenant is not in the seeing. The covenant is in the steps.

There was a whole generation who learned this the hard way. They stood at the edge of a land God had promised them. The land was revealed. The promise was spoken aloud. The hey had come. And they would not seal it with their feet. They looked at the gift and decided the seeing was too risky to walk, and so the promise died with them in the wilderness, not because it was not true, but because a revelation you refuse to walk is a revelation that cannot carry you anywhere. Seeing was never the same as walking. The choice in front of them was never about which feeling they had in their hearts. It was about which direction they pointed their feet.

You are standing where they stood. Maybe you have just seen something true about yourself, more clearly than you have ever seen it. That is the hey, and it is a real gift, and I do not want to rush you past it. But I care about you too much to let you believe the seeing is the finish line. The seeing is the first letter. The walking is the second.

The stake shaped like a tav

There is one more thing you should know about that little crossed-stick letter, and it is the reason any of this is possible.

The tav was a cross long before it was a letter in an alphabet. Two sticks, crossed. The mark. And when the time came, Yeshua was nailed to a stake in that exact shape, secured with three nails to the sign that had carried the meaning of covenant since before there were words to spell. He surrendered to that tav on your behalf. He let Himself be defined entirely by what He came to do for you, and on the other side of that surrender He bought you an identity you could never have earned, sealed in His blood, named in His Word, and meant to be walked out in His Spirit.

So when the Scriptures tell you that anyone in Messiah is a new creation, that the old has gone and the new has come (2 Corinthians 5:17), that is not a poem. That is a covenant He already signed in His own body. He walked His tav so that the truest things about you could be sealed by Him and not by your performance.

He already did the hard part. What He asks of you now is smaller, and yet it is the one thing no one can do for you. Not to earn what you have seen. To live it.

Take the step

So here is the whole teaching in a breath. God shows you who you are. That is the hey, the arms lifted, behold. Then He invites you to walk it, to let the seeing become a sealed and lived thing. That is the tav, the mark, the covenant in the steps. And He has already gone ahead of you and walked the hardest tav there ever was, so that your walk is a response to grace and never a payment for it.

You have seen. That part is done, and it was His doing.

Now point your feet.

The covenant is in the steps.

~ David
