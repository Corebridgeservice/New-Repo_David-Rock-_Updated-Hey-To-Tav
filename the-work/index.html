The work

Four questions, asked in order

Most of us were handed conclusions about who we are before we ever got to ask the question ourselves. The work on this page exists to reverse that. It asks four questions, in an order that matters, and each one gives you something practical to walk out.

Who are you, really? What were you given all of that design to do? When life leans on you, what holds? And how does any of it become the way you actually live? Identity, then direction, then footing, then practice. You can start today with the first one.

The first question

How You’re Made

A Guide to Self

How You’re Made is where discovery becomes practical. It begins with the Guide to Self: a set of questions you answer in your own words, at your own pace, and a written guide that comes back to you, mirroring what your answers reveal about how God designed you specifically. Three sessions. Each one sees you more deeply than the last.

This is not a quiz that hands you a label. Every sentence in your guide is built from what you actually say. People tell me the strangest part is feeling seen by something they wrote themselves. That is the point. The design was already there. The guide helps you see it.

Begin Session 1

The Guide to Self is in beta right now. That means you would be among the early group walking through it, and what you experience helps shape what it becomes. The guide you receive is complete and yours to keep.

The second question

What You’re Built For

Once you have seen how you’re made, a second question opens: what is all of that design for? What You’re Built For walks you back through the dreams you have carried, the ones you set down along the way, and the ones you have not dared to say out loud. Then it helps you name what you actually want your life to produce, in your own words, with nobody grading you.

It moves in three phases: looking back, imagining freely, and making it real. The dreams that surface are not decoration. They are direction, and they make the most sense in the light of your design.

What You’re Built For begins after the Guide to Self. Finish the first question and the road continues from there.

The third question

What You Stand On

Design tells you who you are. Desire tells you where you want to go. Neither holds in a storm without footing. What You Stand On helps you name your convictions, your values, and your operating principles clearly enough to stand on them when standing costs something, and clearly enough to hand them to your children someday.

I am building this one now. If you want to know when it opens, leave your email below and you will be the first to hear.

The fourth question

How You Walk It Out

Seeing clearly is the beginning, not the finish. How You Walk It Out turns what the first three questions reveal into practiced, daily steps: small, structured ways to live what you now know, until the knowing stops being information and starts being how you move through a week.

The first three questions show you the road. This one is where you walk it out. It is on the workbench now, alongside What You Stand On, and the signup below covers both.
